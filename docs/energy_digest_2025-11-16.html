Summary

The article discusses the challenges of maintaining incentive compatibility in virtual power plants (VPPs) that aggregate distributed energy resources (DERs). It proposes a new framework called translation-symmetric market (TSM), which aims to ensure consistent market-clearing processes across all hierarchical levels. The TSM framework promotes transparent valuation of resource contributions and secures competitive conditions.

Key Takeaways

  • The enlarged DER market power within each VPP makes it challenging to maintain incentive compatibility, leading to difficulties in profit allocation procedures.
  • The proposed translation-symmetric market (TSM) framework aims to address this issue by maintaining identical structural forms across all hierarchical levels of the market.
  • TSM preserves service prices across levels and ensures competitive conditions, enabling transparent valuation of resource contributions from DERs.
