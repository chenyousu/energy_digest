FERC Approves PECO-Amazon Transmission Agreement for Pa. Data Center

Source: RTO Insider
Published: November 27, 2025

Summary

The Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC) has approved a transmission security agreement between PECO Energy and Amazon for a data center planned in Falls Township, Pennsylvania. The data center is one of the investments Amazon announced, totaling $20 billion across Pennsylvania. The agreement includes provisions to prevent costs associated with the interconnection from being shifted to other customers.
Read Full Article ‚Üí

Key Takeaways

  • The FERC-approved agreement includes provisions to prevent cost shifting and ensure a committed revenue contribution from Amazon for the data center's load.
  • The ramp schedule outlines when the data center's load is expected to come online, with shortfall payments if milestones aren't reached and termination fees if the load is permanently reduced.
  • The agreement sets a baseline revenue contribution of 80% of what a load-serving entity would pay to serve 80% of the monthly load, subject to a customer shortfall event liability cap.

Understanding Regional Inertia Dynamics in CAISO from Real Grid Disturbances

Source: ArXiv
Published: November 26, 2025

Summary

The article discusses the uneven distribution of power system inertia in California's grid due to the shift from synchronous generators to inverter-based resources. By analyzing real-time frequency data, researchers identified trends in regional inertia and its relationship with renewable generation and battery energy storage systems. The study highlights the importance of monitoring regional inertia to ensure grid reliability amidst growing renewable integration.
Read Full Paper ‚Üí

Key Takeaways

  • Regional RoCoF values were significantly higher than interconnection-wide values, indicating uneven distribution of power system inertia across California's grid.
  • Declining inertia is linked to increasing deployment of battery energy storage systems with synthetic inertia capabilities, which helped mitigate the effects of high rate-of-change frequency.
  • The study emphasizes the need for strategic resource planning and adaptive operational practices to ensure grid reliability in the face of growing renewable integration.

Evaluation of Large Language Models for Numeric Anomaly Detection in Power Systems

Source: ArXiv
Published: November 26, 2025

Summary

This article evaluates the performance of large language models (LLMs) for numeric anomaly detection in power systems. The study uses a representative model, GPT-OSS-20B, on the IEEE 14-bus system and assesses its detection performance and rationale quality across various learning paradigms. The findings provide insights into the limitations and capabilities of LLM-based AD for cyber-physical power grid applications.
Read Full Paper ‚Üí

Key Takeaways

  • The study employs a standardized prompt framework to evaluate LLMs for numeric anomaly detection in power systems, assessing their performance on zero-shot, few-shot, in-context learning, low rank adaptation (LoRA), fine-tuning, and hybrid approaches.
  • A rule-aware design based on the three-sigma criterion is used to assess detection performance and rationale quality, highlighting the potential of LLMs for accurate and interpretable decisions in grid resilience.
  • The research aims to lay the groundwork for further investigation into the limitations and capabilities of LLM-based AD and its integration with classical detectors in cyber-physical power grid applications.
