Semi-Markov Decision Process Framework for Age of Incorrect Information Minimization

Source: ArXiv
Published: December 03, 2025

Summary

Here is the analysis of the power and energy systems article: The article discusses a framework for optimizing age of incorrect information (AoII) minimization in remote estimation systems. It assumes an information source observing a discrete-time finite-state Markov chain and employs push-based transmissions towards a monitor tasked with estimating the source. The goal is to minimize the weighted sum of time average of AoII, estimation, and transmission costs by choosing suitable thresholds.
Read Full Paper ‚Üí

Key Takeaways

  • The article formulates the problem as a semi-Markov decision process (SMDP) with the same state-space as the original discrete-time finite-state Markov chain (DTMC), aiming to optimize multi-threshold transmission policies.
  • A novel stochastic tool called dual-regime absorbing Markov chain (DR-AMC) and its corresponding absorption time distribution (DR-DPH) are used to obtain the optimum parameters for the SMDP, enabling the minimization of AoII costs.
  • The framework aims to minimize the weighted sum of an arbitrary function of AoII and estimation, as well as transmission costs, by choosing optimal thresholds for packet transmissions based on the estimated AoII value.
