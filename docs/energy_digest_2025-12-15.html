Summary

Germany has successfully connected the world's first supercapacitor-based STATCOM (electronic static synchronous compensator) to the grid, marking a significant milestone in power system control technology. The system, developed by Siemens Energy and TenneT, is designed to provide instantaneous reserve and reactive power, a function historically delivered by fossil-fuel generators. This innovation has the potential to revolutionize grid stability and pave the way for a more efficient energy transition.
Read Full Article ‚Üí

Key Takeaways

  • The world's first supercapacitor-based STATCOM system has been connected to the grid in Germany, offering a new approach to providing instantaneous reserve and reactive power.
  • The system, called SVC Plus FS, uses supercapacitors instead of batteries or mechanical inertia, enabling rapid compensation for frequency deviations and delivering very high currents within milliseconds.
  • This innovation is part of a decade-long technology development process, followed by three years of construction, and is positioned as a blueprint to replace fossil-fuel generators in providing grid stability.
