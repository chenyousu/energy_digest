The framework dynamically allocates frequency response among resources based on response speed and available capacity, and has been evaluated using the IEEE 39-bus test system. The results show that synergistic coordination significantly enhances grid stability, especially in low-inertia scenarios.
Read Full Paper ‚Üí

Key Takeaways

  • The proposed coordinated control framework aggregates electric vehicles, data centers, and battery energy storage systems to provide fast frequency response in modern power grids.
  • Dynamic models are developed for each resource type, capturing their response times, power limits, and operational constraints, and a hierarchical control architecture is introduced to allocate FFR among resources effectively.
  • The results show that the coordinated framework improves frequency nadir, reduces RoCoF (renewable oscillation Coefficient of Frequency), and accelerates frequency recovery compared with single-resource FFR, highlighting its value for future frequency regulation markets in renewable-dominated grids.
