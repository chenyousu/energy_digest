Pakistan unveils new net metering rules for rooftop PV

Source: PV Magazine
Published: December 18, 2025

Summary

Pakistan's National Electric Power Regulatory Authority (NEPRA) has launched a public consultation on proposed revisions to its solar net-metering regulations. The changes aim to reduce tariffs for surplus solar power, making the system more competitive and potentially increasing adoption of rooftop PV systems. The new rules will also introduce a new net billing arrangement and shorten the standard net metering agreement term.
Read Full Article ‚Üí

Key Takeaways

  • The proposed reduction in tariff for surplus solar power could cut the rate from PKR 26 ($0.093) per kWh to PKR 13 per kWh, making rooftop PV systems more financially viable.
  • The new rules will introduce a nationally determined average energy purchase price for purchasing surplus power from PV system owners, rather than full one-to-one net credits.
  • The standard net metering agreement term is proposed to be reduced from 7 years to 5 years, with extensions possible by mutual consent of prosumers and utilities.
