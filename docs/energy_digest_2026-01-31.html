Physics Informed Reconstruction of Four-Dimensional Atmospheric Wind Fields Using Multi-UAS Swarm Observations in a Synthetic Turbulent Environment

Source: ArXiv
Published: January 29, 2026

Summary

The article presents a framework for reconstructing four-dimensional atmospheric wind fields using measurements from a coordinated Unmanned Aircraft System (UAS) swarm. A synthetic turbulence environment and high-fidelity multirotor simulation are used to generate training and evaluation data, which is then processed by a physics-informed neural network (PINN) to estimate local wind components and reconstruct a continuous wind field in space and time. The results show that coordinated UAS measurements can accurately recover the dominant spatial and temporal structure of the wind field up to 1000 m altitude.
Read Full Paper ‚Üí

Key Takeaways

  • Accurate reconstruction of atmospheric wind fields is crucial for applications like weather forecasting, hazard prediction, and wind energy assessment, but conventional instruments have spatio-temporal gaps in the lower atmospheric boundary layer.
  • A coordinated UAS swarm can provide flexible in situ measurements, but individual platforms sample wind only along their flight trajectories, limiting full wind-field recovery.
  • The proposed framework uses a bidirectional long short-term memory network (Bi-LSTM) to estimate local wind components and assimilate them into a physics-informed neural network (PINN) to reconstruct a continuous wind field in space and time.
