EPRI Suggests Path to Limit Grid Costs of Data Center Surge

Source: RTO Insider
Published: February 01, 2026

Summary

The Energy Policy Institute (EPRI) suggests that the rapid addition of large loads to the grid, such as data centers, does not necessarily have to raise electricity rates. In fact, if the incremental costs of serving these new loads are below the current average costs, they can actually lower the average retail rates for all customers on the grid, provided there is excess grid capacity or a relatively cheap source of new electrons.
Read Full Article ‚Üí

Key Takeaways

  • If the incremental costs of serving new loads, such as data centers, are below the current average costs, they can potentially lower the average retail rates for all customers on the grid.
  • However, if expensive grid investments are needed to serve these new loads or if the demand from these loads reduces before those investments are paid for, prices could rise for other customers on the grid.
  • The key to achieving a "win-win" scenario is to have planning, pricing structures, and flexible demand in place to manage the growing electricity needs of data centers and other large loads.
  • The study suggests that understanding how load growth interacts with system costs is crucial in shaping the future energy landscape.
