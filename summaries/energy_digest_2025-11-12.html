California legislation sets new safety standards for battery storage

Source: PV Magazine
Published: November 12, 2025

Summary

California Governor Gavin Newsom has enacted Senate Bill 283, a fire safety-focused legislation that requires energy storage developers to meet with local fire authorities before and after installation. The bill aims to reshape the state's energy storage deployment by prioritizing safety as part of system design and operation. This move is seen as a response to the Moss Landing fire in February 2025, which led to emergency regulations.
Read Full Article ‚Üí

Key Takeaways

  • California's Senate Bill 283 introduces proactive documentation and data-driven safety compliance for battery storage developers, shifting their focus from hardware redesign to safety measures.
  • The bill requires developers to meet with local fire authorities before submitting project applications and after installation, aligning project design with safety standards such as NFPA 855.
  • The legislation underscores the growing need to prioritize safety as a key part of energy storage system design and operation, recognizing that system risks evolve over time.

German network operators approve grid connection of 46 GWh of storage at medium voltage level

Source: PV Magazine
Published: November 12, 2025

Summary

The German Federal Network Agency (BNetzA) has published figures on connection requests for battery energy storage systems planned for medium-voltage commissioning. In 2024, network operators received 9,710 connection requests totaling 400 GW/661 GWh, indicating a growing demand for grid-connected storage in Germany. This trend is expected to continue with the approval of large-scale battery storage projects worth 51 GW.
