Poland raises permit-free energy storage limit to 30 kWh

Source: PV Magazine
Published: November 13, 2025

Summary

Poland's lower house of parliament, the Sejm, has passed amendments to the Building Law, removing the requirement for building permits for energy storage facilities with capacities of up to 30 kWh. This change aims to simplify procedures for small-scale commercial and industrial projects. The new regulation will have a positive impact on the development of small-scale energy storage systems.

Key Takeaways

  • Poland has raised its permit-free energy storage limit from 20 kWh to 30 kWh, simplifying procedures for standalone installations.

  • The change is a positive step towards encouraging renewable energy adoption and reducing reliance on traditional energy sources.
