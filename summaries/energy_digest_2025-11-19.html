US utility-scale solar delays ease as market stabilizes

Source: PV Magazine
Published: November 19, 2025

Summary

The US Energy Information Administration (EIA) reports that the number of delayed utility-scale solar projects has decreased, indicating stability in the US solar pipeline. The share of delayed projects fell from 25% to 20% of planned capacity between 2024 and the third quarter of 2025. This decline suggests improvements in project timelines, permitting, supply chains, and financing.

Key Takeaways

  • The number of delayed utility-scale solar projects has decreased, with the share falling from 25% to 20% of planned capacity between 2024 and the third quarter of 2025.
  • Despite a high number of delays in 2024, the year still saw a significant increase in installed capacity, with 31 GW of new utility-scale solar brought online, representing a 34% increase in cumulative US installed capacity.
  • Most delayed projects occur during late construction or testing phases, often resulting in postponements of one or two months, but are less common than cancellations, which typically account for less than 1% of planned capacity.

Source: ArXiv
Published: November 18, 2025

Summary

The article discusses the development of a unified pipeline to recover AC feasible solutions from DCOPF-based dispatches, which are often infeasible under practical operating scenarios. The pipeline uses four DCOPF variants and employs distributed slack allocation and PV/PQ switching to improve AC feasibility. The results show significant improvements in solution quality compared to conventional methods.

Key Takeaways

  • The unified pipeline improves AC feasibility by reducing inequality constraint violations, with a factor of 3 to 5 under extreme loading conditions.
