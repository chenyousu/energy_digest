Spatially Dependent Sampling of Component Failures for Power System Preventive Control Against Hurricane

Source: ArXiv
Published: November 20, 2025

Summary

The article discusses a new approach to modeling spatial dependence in component failures of power systems during hurricanes. The proposed method generates correlated meteorological intensity random variables, which enables joint sampling of multiple component failures. This approach captures long-tailed scenarios and reveals more extreme events than conventional methods, improving the realism of scenario generation for preventive control.
Read Full Paper ‚Üí

Key Takeaways

  • Strong spatial correlations in weather intensity lead to interdependent component failures, regardless of their mean value level.
  • The proposed spatially dependent sampling method uncovers more high-severity scenarios that are missed by independent sampling.
  • Preventive control requires balancing load curtailment and over-generation costs under different scenario severities, and ignoring failure correlations can result in underestimated risk from high-severity events.
