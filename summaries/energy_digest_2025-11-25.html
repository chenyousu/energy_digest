When solar meets next-gen nuclear

Source: PV Magazine
Published: November 25, 2025

Summary

Here is the analysis of the article: Scientists in China have proposed a novel scheduling framework for microgrids that integrates solar power with small modular nuclear reactors to improve short-term dispatch capability and long-term economic viability. The framework uses multi-objective distributionally robust optimization with real-time reinforcement learning, resulting in an 18.7% reduction in operational costs. This innovative approach combines multiple energy sources under a single optimization paradigm.
Read Full Article ‚Üí

Key Takeaways

  • The proposed system utilizes a hybrid energy management framework that integrates solar power and small modular nuclear reactors to provide complementary power sources.
  • A multi-objective distributionally robust optimization (DRO) is employed, combined with real-time reinforcement learning (RL), to generate baseline scheduling strategies and continuously adjust control signals for improved adaptability.
  • The system also incorporates auxiliary resources such as generators, batteries, electrolyzers, and an energy management system (EMS) to manage short-term and long-term storage dynamics and optimize hydrogen production and utilization.

Impact Analysis of COVID-19 in Bangladesh Power Sector and Recommendations based on Practical Data and Machine Learning Approach

Source: ArXiv
Published: November 24, 2025

Summary

The article investigates the impact of COVID-19 on Bangladesh's power sector, analyzing changes in load patterns and system stability. The study uses machine learning techniques to predict load profiles and assess the effectiveness of various measures. It provides recommendations for overcoming future disasters by improving energy efficiency, investing in renewable energy, and developing more resilient power systems.
Read Full Paper ‚Üí

Key Takeaways

  • The power demand in Bangladesh decreased by approximately 15.4% and 17.2% in April and May 2020 compared to the same periods in 2019, indicating a significant impact of COVID-19 on the country's power sector.
  • Machine learning models, such as Long-Short-Term Memory (LSTM) frameworks, were used to predict load profiles and compare them with actual values, revealing an average power demand decrease of approximately 19.5% in April 2020 and 18.3% in May 2020 relative to projected values.
  • The study highlights the importance of developing more resilient power systems, investing in renewable energy, and improving energy efficiency to mitigate the impact of future disasters on the power sector.
